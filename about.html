Our Story

Chris Schults, Managing Partner of a cabinetry showroom in Ann Arbor, was trying to solve a familiar problem: leads were coming in, but response times were inconsistent, follow-up depended on who was available, and the only obvious ways to grow were hiring more people or spending more on ads.

Instead, Chris shut off his ads and built an internal system to respond seamlessly using AI calls, texts, and emails. It moved the needle 5-10%. Conversations improved. Opportunities stopped slipping through the cracks.

Once the system proved itself on inbound, curiosity took over. What if it didn't just respond to demand — what if it could help create it?

To answer that question, Chris teamed up with Emil Halili, Jude Telan, and Ben Lorenzo — founders of the largest AI automation community in the Philippines. Together, they began scaling the system and turning it outbound.

AI unlocked speed and reach, but it also exposed a flaw. At scale, pure AI lacked supervision, coaching, and control. Without structure, consistency was at risk.

The Breakthrough

That tension led to the breakthrough. CallView was built as a human-in-the-loop AI calling platform — a command center where AI handles volume and humans stay in control. Sales reps can supervise multiple AI callers at once, monitor conversations live, coach in real-time, and step into calls when judgment matters most.

Then came the moment that changed everything. The system began generating more qualified trade pro conversations than the team could handle. The bottleneck shifted from lead generation to needing about 20 more designers.

What started as an internal fix became something much bigger — and far bigger than cabinetry.

Today, CallView helps teams scale outbound without losing consistency, control, or their human voice. It exists because it was needed — and because the future of growth isn't human or AI, but both working together.
